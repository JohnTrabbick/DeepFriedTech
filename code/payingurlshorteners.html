The internet world has made plenty of new technologies and upgrades to its circle. One of those technologies concerning link/URL shorteners is a variant that pays you cash for every click you get on your URL.

It sounds nice, doesn't it? Just convert a URL, share it on social media, group chat, etc., And boom! Free cash! It all sounds very irresistable. Though, it can't just appear, it needs a source, and the source for all of those $$$ comes from advertisements.

Even with the ads to generate the revenue, you still may earn very little from using URL shorteners. For an example;

The revenue of a single click on an ad link. Believe it or not, thats a really high amount from a single click!

Other concerns with using paying URL shorteners is that malware and NFSW ads are commonly present on some/most URL shorteners to barely stabilize publisher and company profits. Thus, on the huge downside, NFSW and malware ads can easily turn away visitors to your links. Owners of paying shorteners are also known to usually be inactive and even corrupt the system, seeing their website as an easy way to profit, considering that the majority of owners usually use copy-paste websites (which is actually true).

If you are really on a tight budget or if you do't really care (you should really care though), here's a list of the top 5 shorteners.

1. Adf.ly

 

The payout of a single click on Adf.ly is $0.0025.

Adf.ly is the pioneer of paying URL shorteners. Started in 2009, Adf.ly maintains good payout rates and high stability, making it attractive to users who prefer a veteran in paying URL shortener services, providing consistent payout rates.

SUMMARY:

Adf.ly is a stable paying URL shortener that is the first to exist of it's kind.

Adf.ly contains less malware and NFSW ads in comparison with other competitors, but it is still prevalent.

Adf.ly's interface is very dated.

Upon interstital exit, there is a chance for Adf.ly to redirect. The redirects contain scam ads.

2. Link1s.com

 

The payout for a single click on Link1s is $0.0080.

Link1s.com is a Vietnamese-based paying shortener that has a variety of opportunities for publishers. You can tweak how much ad pages are displayed, giving full control over publisher options on how much they want on their links. The payouts of Link1s.com is also high, albeit a little more volatile than other competitors such as Adf.ly.

SUMMARY:

Link1s gives you the option to use multiple ad pages (up to 4 pages), enabling you to receive higher profits.

Link1s has a modern, navigatable interface.

Link1s pays out fixed payments, altered overtime depending on revenues.

Link1s has a download pop-up at the end of the interstital ad session, which brews potential for malware. However, all other ads are supplied by Google Adsense, so NFSW and malware ads are otherwise nonexistent/minor.

3. Adfoc.us

 

The payout of a single click on Adfoc.us is $0.0003.

Adfoc.us is another paying shortener that has been recently falling out of style as a paying shortener. Living over 10 years since writing, Adfoc.us has shown to be significantly afflicted by it's aging. Development for new features have come to a halt, and payout rates are getting bottom-low. However, it is worth noting that Adfoc.us gives cash bonuses to game content creators especially those coming from Minecraft. They also feature link customization if you get popular enough.

SUMMARY:

Adfoc.us development has shriveled up.

Game content creators are eligible to receive FREE cash bonuses.

You can customize your shortened links on Adfoc.us once you reach a threshold.

Adfoc.us will frequently not display ads in error. This cuts off the point of paying users, owing up to it's abysmal payouts.

4. BC.VC

 

BC.VC does not appearing to be registering profits upon clicks.

BC.VC is a paying shortener focused on being modern. As such, when you first step onto the site it will look up-to date and maintained, and it has useful API such as their reports, which give you a bunch of detail on how yours links are doing, including geo-maps. However, BC.VC is having a tight time with ad spaces, and as such there will not be much ads. If there are ads, its mostly just a "Download!" button or a redirect to a random website. Due to the lack of ads, you should expect very low profits. Perhaps in the future when it gets more advertisers, it might be "worth" checking out.

SUMMARY:

BC.VC greatly suffers from lack of advertisers.

BC.VC's shortlinks are quick and fast, making it so that your users don't get so much a hassle dealing with ad shortlinks.

Since BC.VC suffers from a lack of advertisers, they must make out the most of their profits using malicious ads and redirects. The redirects are fortunately not too sketchy, they usually fail to load or redirect to a known website, like Cex.io or a Xiaomi phone seller. However, it is not perfect, so don't say we didn't warn you!

5. Ouo.io

Ouo.io is a decent paying shortener that aims to be simple yet profitable. Their interface is similar to BC.VC's, without the detailed statistics. Upon clicking on a shortlink, it appear they use two ad providers, Adskeeper and Runative, which both permit NFSW ads. Unfortunately enough, alot of advertisers are advertising NFSW ads. Alongside with that, they do not appear to be registering payouts. We tried spoofing different IP's 5 times, but a single payout hasn't appeared. It also appear that they are making fake reviews on their official blog..

Rounding it up, paying shorteners aren't just worth it overall. Malicious and NFSW ads, penny payouts, and the habit of the owners idling over issues are just a few reasons why you shouldn't use shorteners. Just DON'T use them unless if you are really on a tight budget.
