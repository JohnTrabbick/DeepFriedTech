Image hosting is practically the same thing as cloud storage, but it is more optimized torwards, well, images! It differs from cloud storage by offering storage dedicated to image formats such as PNG or JPG, giving users options such as HTML image embeds or forum embedding. Usually, image hosts also feature a place where people can see other people's images, something like viewing social media posts. Infact, its uncommon for an image host to also function as an image forum, such as Imgur.com.

In this article, I will be viewing free image hosts by checking out the user dashboard, see trustpilot details, etc.

1. Google Photos

Google Photos is an image host that is quite simple. You get a sidebar menu, a place to view your photos, and even a place to print and deliever your photos. You can store up to 15GB with the free plan, however, the storage is shared with other Google services such as Gmail or Google Drive, and a Google account is required to use this service.

SUMMARY

Simple interface.

15GB can be quite generous for free, however, your storage is shared with other Google services such as Gmail or Google Drive.

It's Google. Google is a big web giant, it can provide a lot of resources to it's web services, but Google is also known for tampering with a lot of your data, storing it for undefined times.

For website owners, Google Photos doesn't provide an option to embed images in HTML.

 

2. Imgur

Imgur is another free image host, which also functions as an image board. It provides links to a variety of social media platforms, and provides embed links for HTML and forums. Currently, it doesn't seem to impose any storage caps. Imgur makes profit via displaying advertisements from Google, it also alternatively makes profit by it's membership program.

Embed link/link section.

Imgur image dashboard.

SUMMARY:

It currently seems that Imgur doesn't hold any storage caps on images stored.

It uses Google, and it tracks stuff from you itself. However, it collects data that can't be used to identify you personally. According to the privacy policy, they collect data such as operating system type, IP address, and browser type. They share this data with 3rd party advertiser and analytic companies, but they forbid them by a contract to combine this data with other data to personally identify you. They also state in their policy that they use and collect this data to allow them to be compatible with copyright laws, and that they can give data to entities such as the court, national defense, and law enforcement. In summary, they collect non-identifiable data and use or share this data responsibly.

For those who tumble around social media, own a website, or use forums, Imgur provides links to embed/share images easily.

Imgur utilizes advertising and captivating membership perks to sustain and grow beyond it's business/website bills.

3. Postimages

Postimages is another image host that has a simple interface. Unlike the two previous image hosts, Postimage does not require an account in order to use their services. Currently, Postimages doesn't seem to impose a storage cap, similar to Imgur, but it does mention "Unlimited Image Hosting" as a benefit on it's premium membership page, however, it might just be an expression. It also features a variety of embed links including forum embeds.

Landing page.

Image dashboard.

SUMMARY:

Intuitive interface.

Postimage doesn't clearly specify whether or not if they have storage caps.

Postimages's privacy policy states that they use cookies for 3 things, save user preferences, track advertising, and make aggregated data on things such as site traffic and interactions. Postimages does NOT share data with 3rd parties, but exclusions are given to parties that help operate the website, such as Google Ads or Postimages's web host, so long as they promise to keep the data confidential. Otherwise, Postimages doesn't collect much data.

Postimage pays off business bills via ads and it's membership program.
