Chrome is the most popular browser in the world, holding more than 70% market share in 2021. Being maintained by Google, it actively receives updates and is well maintained, sealed away from most security bugs. However, Chrome is also known for major flaws, including tampering with a lot of your data.

The first pro on this list is that Chrome is owned and backed by Google. Google constantly mantains Chrome and there is currently no sign of development chugging down. With bug fixes and new features being added all year long, its probably one of the many reasons why Chrome is used.

 

The first con with using chrome is that it carries tracking abilities which are quite intrusive. Not only it may slow down your browsing experience, but Google collects quite alot of data which they store. This includes your IP address, browsing activities, and much more. Infact, Google hands over alot of data to 3rd parties, including governments, and they can even track down your location!

 

The second con of using chrome is that it is infamous for being a resource hog. While Chrome has improved alot since back then, it still consumes quite a chunk of resources. As seen in the pictures below, the memory usage of Chrome isn't quite pleasing.

Browser comparison of memory usage with 9 tabs open.

The second pro of Google Chrome is that you get a pretty fast browser. While its not guranteed that one browser is always the best, Chrome still scores some blowing speeds. Thanks to rigorous testing and constant benchmark tests done by Google, it has been shaped to have fast and consistent speeds.

Browser speed benchmark.

Google Chrome syncs Google's services such as Gmail with your account when you sign in. This can be really convenient for some people who frequently hop around a lot of Google's services. However, when signing into Google Chrome, you should be prepared for Google to track down much more information about you and store them, not in bulk, but as a personalized profile!
SUMMARY

 

Google Chrome is a stable, well-maintained, and reliable browser for everyday use. However, it consumes a little too much resources for simple browsing, and all of your activities on Chrome is stored and shared by Google.
